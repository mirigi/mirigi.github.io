Mirigi draws the building's existing systems into a **single, composed experience**. The resident's **smart-home controller**, the **RFID access layer** and the **vehicle workflow** converge into *one app for residents and one console for staff*. Nothing new sits on the wall, nothing extra in a drawer. The intelligence is in **how the pieces speak to one another**.

Inside the app, a built-in **Home Automation module** operates each unit's connected devices. Controllers expose **named commands for lights, climate, blinds and scenes**, grouped by zone, alongside **macros that fire several commands with a single tap**. Replies from the controller are parsed through configurable templates, so the app reflects *real state* — setpoint, fan mode, on or off — rather than guessing. Traffic is routed through a **per-building automation proxy**, keeping the resident's controller **off the public internet**. A wide range of **third-party controllers** can be wired in, and a first-class **Khimo integration** pairs securely to import each unit's zones directly. **Miri**, the in-app assistant, can execute the same commands and macros on request.

For staff, the console manages the building's **RFID fob database**, **bulk-loaded from CSV**, tied to units and residents, **searchable and revocable from one screen**. **Per-fob limits** keep physical access aligned with residency and amenity rules. The **vehicle module** tracks each unit's cars and their *parked or requested status*, and becomes the natural surface for **garage-gate workflows**: a resident requests their car, the concierge sees it live, and any gate exposed as a command is triggered from the same flow. Units are organised into **elevator groups and floors**, so maintenance notices and announcements reach only the risers affected. Requests, vehicle movements, fob changes and automation actions live in **one place**, with **remote visibility** that lets the team resolve issues *before residents notice them*.
