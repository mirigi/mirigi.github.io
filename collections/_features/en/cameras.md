A quick glance at the **pool deck** before heading down. A look at the **lobby** when the doorbell rings. A peek at the **beachfront** before stepping out. Mirigi's Cameras feature brings **shared spaces into the same app** residents already use for messages, deliveries and amenity bookings — *no separate surveillance tool, no extra login, no learning curve*. Each camera lives as its **own page** inside the building's concierge, **one tap away** from everything else that matters.

Mirigi connects to the **IP cameras already installed** in the building. It handles both **continuous video streams** and **auto-refreshing still images** that update on a **configurable interval**, keeping the feature usable on *older browsers* and **low-bandwidth connections** that cannot sustain a live stream. Each camera page picks the right view **automatically** for the resident's device — phone, tablet, lobby terminal — so the experience stays smooth without any manual setup.

Buildings can publish **as many camera pages as needed** — pool-cam, beach-cam, lobby-cam, parking entrance, playroom — each configured independently with its own **name** and **ordering**. A **per-device visibility rule** lets the building hide a camera on screens where streaming is not appropriate, such as *very low-power in-unit panels*, while **phones, tablets and computers** retain full access.

Cameras live alongside every other page in the building's concierge, sharing the same **navigation, look and feel** as deliveries, messages and amenity bookings — no extra app to install, no separate credentials, no surveillance UI to learn.
