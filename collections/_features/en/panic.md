The **Panic Button** feature in Mirigi gives every resident a **quiet, dependable way to summon help** the moment something goes wrong. With a **single tap**, a medical, security or fire emergency is raised inside the same app residents already use for messages, reservations and deliveries — *no separate device, no learning curve*. Behind that tap is a **building-wide response plan** that activates instantly, so households know help is already on its way before they have finished explaining what happened. The result is the kind of **peace of mind** that defines a premium residence.

Mirigi supports **fire, security and medical** emergencies out of the box, and each building chooses which types to enable and which **staff roles** receive each alert — security, front desk, management or any custom group. Notification text and staff message templates are localized in **English, Spanish, Portuguese and Russian**, so every alert reaches each team member in their working language. To prevent accidental alarms, triggering the button is a **two-step action**: tap the emergency type, then confirm. Once confirmed, **only the resident can cancel** the request — until a staff member picks it up, at which point it becomes *being handled by* that staff member and is locked in for resolution.

For staff, alerts arrive in **real time as push notifications** with full context: unit, resident, emergency type, and the household's registered emergency contact. Residents can pre-register a **personal emergency contact** — a family member, doctor or trusted neighbor — whose details attach automatically to every alert, tailoring the response to each home. As soon as one staff member **acknowledges** the request, the emergency moves into *being handled* and the resident sees exactly who is responding — at that point only staff can close it. Like every Mirigi capability, the Panic Button is **optional and fully configurable** by building administrators.
