Mirigi **replaces the lobby corkboard** and the under-the-door circular with a **refined digital channel** that reaches every resident on their **phone or in-unit touch panel**. Announcements arrive *instantly*, look as polished on screen as they did on paper, and **disappear the moment they stop being relevant**. The result is a quieter building, a **lighter environmental footprint**, and a management office freed from the logistics of printing and distribution.

For staff, the **messaging console** offers **precise targeting** and effortless authorship. A notice can be addressed to **every home, a single elevator line, specific floors, or a hand-picked list of units**, and is composed with a subject, a rich-text body and an **expiration date** so outdated information clears itself automatically. Messages are written in a full editor with **Markdown formatting and drag-and-drop images**, ideal for *circulars, bylaws excerpts, expense summaries or meeting minutes*. An integrated **AI assistant** turns a brief prompt into a polished draft, and a built-in **translation assistant** carries the subject and body from the primary language into **every other locale the building supports with a single click**. Any notice can also be flagged for **public display**, mirroring it on lobby memo-board screens alongside the personal delivery to each resident's app.

For residents, every message arrives in **their own language**, with an **unread counter** and a clean, *searchable inbox*. Staff retain a **paginated, searchable archive** of past communications, with **seen/unseen tracking per unit** — the *institutional memory of the building*, always one query away.
