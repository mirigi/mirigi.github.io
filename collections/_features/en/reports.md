Every building runs differently, and **every report Mirigi produces is tailored to the property**. The catalog is **fully customizable per building**, so the management team can ask any question that matters to them — *notifications per month*, *reservations per month*, *packages per day*, *visitors by floor*, *late check-ins on weekends* — and get the answer in a clean, board-ready format. What used to be a guessing game at the annual meeting becomes a calm conversation backed by numbers, *tuned to how this building actually operates*.

From the staff console, the team runs any report **on demand** with the parameters it requires — typically a **start and end date**, but any input the report needs is supported. Each result can be inspected as a **table** for line-by-line review or as a **chart** for at-a-glance trends, switched with a single tap. The same data is **downloadable as CSV** for follow-up work in any spreadsheet. Reports are organised into **groups** chosen by the building — *notifications, reservations, deliveries, vehicles, requests*, or any other group the property defines — and there's a **Run all in this group** view that fires every report at once for a quick operational dashboard.

For **staff and operations**, the impact is *practical decisions backed by evidence*. Reservation patterns highlight amenities that are over- or under-used; delivery volumes flag when the package room is filling up; activity reports show where time is really being spent. When a board member asks a new question, the answer can be **added to the catalog and kept** — once defined, available forever.

And for **residents**, the impact is felt even when they never open a chart: a building that *understands its own rhythm*, anticipates peak periods, and invests in the amenities that actually get used.
