Every great arrival begins before the doorbell rings. Mirigi's **Visitor Authorization** turns the lobby into a *seamless extension of each residence*, where **expected guests are welcomed by name** and unexpected ones are handled with poise. Residents **pre-register family, friends, service providers and clients** from the app — or simply ask Miri in plain language — and the front desk sees exactly who is allowed in, when, and on whose behalf. The result is a quieter entrance, a calmer staff, and a building that feels *effortlessly private*.

Authorizations adapt to real life. A guest can be cleared **always**, **never**, or within a **precise time window**, with **one-off, weekly or monthly recurrences** bounded by start and end dates. Residents register a visitor's name, identification, phone and email, with **duplicate IDs detected per unit** to prevent double entries. Recurring guests — a weekend sibling, a Tuesday housekeeper, a regular client — are *set up once and remembered*. With Miri, a sentence like "my cousin is coming for dinner on Friday" is enough; the **AI concierge** captures the details, time window and recurrence, and files the authorization automatically.

For arrivals by car, the **visitor valet** registers the guest vehicle under a **ticket number** and sends an **SMS**, or offers a **QR code**, with a link to request the car back. *No app, no account, no password* — the valet is notified the instant the visitor is ready to leave.

Staff gain an equally refined toolset. The **authorized list** is searchable by **name, identification or unit**, and **walk-ins can be registered on the spot**. Every **check-in and check-out is logged** against the guest and the staff member who cleared it, producing an *auditable arrivals history* that residents and administrators can review at any time. Rules are evaluated in **real time** against the building's local timezone, so the lobby always knows precisely who belongs inside.
