Service Level Agreement (SLA)

1 Introduction

The Seller shall use all reasonable commercial efforts to ensure that the Service is operating and available to Customers 99% of the time in any calendar month. In the event Customer experiences any of the service performance issues defined below due to the Sellers failure to provide Services, Customer will be eligible to receive the Service Credits described below.

2 Definitions.

The following definitions shall apply to the SLA

2.0.1 "Downtime" means if there is more than a ten percent user error rate. Downtime is measured based on server side error rate.

2.0.2 "Downtime Period" means, for a domain, if a period of fifteen consecutive minutes of Downtime. Intermittent Downtime for a period of less than fifteen minutes will not be counted towards any Downtime Periods.

2.0.3 "Monthly Uptime Percentage" means total number of minutes in a calendar month minus the number of minutes of Downtime suffered from all Downtime Periods in a calendar month, divided by the total number of minutes in a calendar month.

2.0.4 "Scheduled Downtime" means those times where the Seller notifies Customers of periods of Downtime two days prior to the commencement of such Downtime. There will be no more than twenty four hours of Scheduled Downtime per calendar year. Scheduled Downtime is not considered Downtime for purposes of this SLA, and will not be counted towards any Downtime Periods.

2.0.5 "Service" means the Seller's service provided by the Seller to You under the Agreement.

2.0.6 "Service Credit" means: (a) seven days of Service added to the end of Your term for the Service, at no charge to You, if the Monthly Uptime Percentage for any calendar month is between 99.0% and 95.0 %; or (b) fifteen days of Service added to the end of Your term for the Service, at no charge to You, if the Monthly Uptime Percentage for any calendar month is less than 95.0%.

2.1 Customer Must Request Service Credit.

2.1.1 In order to receive any of the Service Credits described above, Customer must notify the Seller within thirty (30) days from the time Customer becomes eligible to receive a Service Credit.

2.1.2 Failure to comply with this requirement will forfeit Customers right to receive a Service Credit.

2.2 Maximum Service Credit.

2.2.1 The aggregate maximum number of Service Credits to be issued by the Seller to Customer for any and all Downtime Periods that occur in a single calendar month shall not exceed fifteen days of Service added to the end of Your term for the Service.

2.2.2 Service Credits may not be exchanged for, or converted to, monetary compensation.

2.3 | SLA Exclusions.

2.3.1 The SLA does not apply to any service(s) that expressly exclude this SLA (as stated in the documentation for such services) and any performance issues: (i) caused by factors outside of the Sellers reasonable control; (ii) that resulted from any actions or inactions of Customer or any third parties; (iii) that resulted from Customers equipment and/or third party equipment (not within the primary control of the Seller); or (iv) that resulted from third party providers to the Seller (for example SMS forwarders or e-mail services).

2.3.2 This SLA states Customer's sole and exclusive remedy for any failure by the Seller to provide the Service.
